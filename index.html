About Me

I'm a first-year PhD student in the EECS department at UC Berkeley. After finishing my undergrad at Cal and a stint working on campus as research staff, I've decided that Berkeley is a nice enough place to push my stay from my current total of seven years to many more.

Research

I am advised by Prof. Jonathan Bachrach, and am happy to be able to work with the excellent group of graduate students in Berkeley's ASPIRE Lab.

My work currently spans several areas of computer architecture. I spend most of my time working on the design of Hurricane, a manycore architecture for efficient implementation of DSP systems. This project incorporates data-parallel features that build on the Hwacha project, a novel vector-fetch architecture from UC Berkeley.
